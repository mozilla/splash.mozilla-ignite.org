About the challenge

Your mission: Build apps that can't work on today's Internet.

Apps From The Future

Say you have an idea about how to make people happier, or healthier, or more well informed—but that idea is too big for today's internet.

Transfer speeds are too slow, latency is too high, or it's computationally unfeasible. Or, maybe you need to create your own protocol. Whatever the reason, the naysayers will tell you that it can't be built.

Now imagine that none of these limits apply. Information can travel as fast as it needs to. "Loading" and "latency" are things thing of the past. And as an app developer, you can program the entire network, from the clients and servers to all the middle boxes in between.

What would you build?

You don't need to constrain your imagination. Don't be afraid to pull from the Jetsons. Imagine:

  • Zero-latency telepresence. Teaching, collaborating in real time to students around the world. Remote surgery.
  • New forms of peer-to-peer communities for ambient intelligence and monitoring.
  • Whole new forms of distributed computing. Donating computing cycles to find a cure for cancer or discover intelligent life in space. 

    • The Mozilla Ignite apps challenge invites you to spend some time in the future—and build some apps there.

We need you

Sure, we're making it easy to access funding for your world-changing app. But don't think of this as just a competition.

Mozilla Ignite is your chance to be a pioneer, to collaborate with cutting edge thinkers and tech, and to help catalyze radical thinking about a radical new horizon. 

Ideas challenge

Share your ideas and refine them in a big public brainstorm. Join us at salons and hack days around the U.S.

Development challenge

Assemble a team and start building—we're making it easy to get your idea off the ground, with learning resources, mentorship, and $500,000 in funding.

With your help, we will prototype ways that the next generation of networks can drive urban renewal, innovation, and public benefit—and shape the future of the Internet as a public resource.
